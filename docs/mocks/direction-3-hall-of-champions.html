Rationale

Thirty years of Quake deserves a trophy room, not a spreadsheet. This direction frames the leaderboard as the definitive historical record: a spotlight gradient over near-black, champagne-and-bronze metals instead of saturated paint, generous row height, and a podium strip that puts the top three under glass before the full table begins. Medal headers become understated metal-underlined labels and the placement counts themselves carry the metal color — the system survives, whispered rather than shouted. The floating pill toolbar keeps filter chrome out of the composition. This is the direction that best serves the “all-time GOAT list” framing and shares beautifully on social; the trade-off is density (taller rows) and one honest flag: the podium strip is the single element beyond pure theming — a small presentational block at the top of PlayerList rendering rows 1–3 from data it already has. Drop the podium and the direction remains fully theme-only.

Implementation notes (MUI mapping)

  • Theme: Sora + Orbitron, shape.borderRadius: 14, palette primary = champagne #d6b25e; spotlight = one radial-gradient on body (true global in the slimmed App.css).
  • Medal classes keep their names; headers switch from filled bars to color + 2px underline, placement cells to whisper tints + metal-colored numerals — all in the existing CSS classes / theme overrides.
  • Flagged deviation: the podium strip is a new presentational block at the top of PlayerList (slices rows 1–3 it already computed). ~40 lines of JSX, no state or logic changes — but it is beyond a pure theme swap. The direction works without it.
  • Pill toolbar = MUI Paper with full radius + Chips; the year mini-slider opens the real slider in a popover.
  • Row height via MuiTableCell padding override; density costs ~35% more scroll than direction 1 — deliberate.
